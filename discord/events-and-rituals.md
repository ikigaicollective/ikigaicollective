# Events & Rituals

A server feels alive when members know roughly what happens and when. These recurring rituals reinforce the studio feel and give members reliable moments of connection.

Start with one or two. Don't schedule five things and watch four of them become ghost-events. Add as the community fills each one out.

## Weekly

### Lab Night — the anchor event

- **Format.** Stage channel. Members present what they built, measured, or iterated on that week. 3–5 minutes each. Open floor for questions.
- **Rhythm.** Same day, same time, every week. Published in `#events-calendar` with time-zone-aware rotation if the membership spans regions.
- **Why this matters.** The "publish your lab notes" practice needs a deadline rhythm to stick. Lab Night is that deadline.
- **Host.** `@Event Facilitator`, rotating. A sign-up sheet (Sesh) opens 24h before.
- **Archive.** Record (with consent), transcribe, post to `#ama-archive`.

### Open co-working block

- **Format.** A 90-minute `Focused Sprint` voice session. Opens with a 2-minute check-in (what are you working on?), then silent working, then a 10-minute closing share.
- **Rhythm.** Twice a week, rotated across time zones so `@Americas`, `@EMEA`, `@APAC` each get one reachable slot.
- **Host.** Optional — can be leader-less.

## Bi-weekly or monthly

### Office Hours

- **Format.** One member volunteers to hold space in a voice channel for 45 minutes. Anyone can drop in with questions or work to review.
- **Rhythm.** Sign up for a slot on a shared calendar — no pressure to fill every week. Let it be organic.

### Domain Jam

- **Format.** A single domain channel (`#art-design`, `#code-engineering`, etc.) hosts a themed jam — 1 week, 1 prompt, members respond with outputs.
- **Rhythm.** Monthly. Rotate which domain hosts.
- **Why.** Keeps domain channels from becoming dormant. Gives a low-stakes creative prompt.

## Quarterly

### Reflection Night

- **Format.** A live session where members share: one experiment that worked, one that didn't, and what they're designing next.
- **Rhythm.** End of each quarter.
- **Why.** Reinforces the full Design → Implement → Measure → Iterate loop as a lived practice, not just a diagram.

### Community Ops Town Hall

- **Format.** Open discussion of the server itself: what's working, what's cluttered, what's missing.
- **Rhythm.** Quarterly.
- **Why.** Prevents structural bloat. This is where channel consolidation, role pruning, and bot reviews happen.

## Ad-hoc rituals

Things that aren't on a calendar but should feel "available":

- **Welcome threads.** Every `#introductions` post auto-creates a thread. Onboarding buddies (and anyone else) drop in to say hi.
- **"I shipped" emoji reaction.** Unique custom emoji for when someone posts a launch. Light ceremony. No XP, no leaderboard.
- **Quiet hours.** An unspoken norm: `@Announcements` pings aren't used on weekends.
- **Season breaks.** Pause recurring events for 1–2 weeks per year (summer, late December). Say so in advance. Prevents burnout.

## What *not* to schedule

- Daily anything. Daily obligations turn into guilt very quickly in a voluntary community.
- Mandatory events. Nothing is mandatory here.
- Competitions or leaderboards. Conflicts with flat structure.

## Calendar setup

- One source of truth: the events bot (Sesh/Apollo). Everything flows to `#events-calendar`.
- Time zone handling: bot posts in UTC + the originator's local; members see their own time zone in the bot's UI.
- RSVP: optional. Lower pressure = higher attendance.
- Reminder pings: 1 hour before start, only to members who RSVP'd yes.
