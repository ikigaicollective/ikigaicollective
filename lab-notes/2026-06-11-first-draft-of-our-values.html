First Draft of Our Values

Week 8 · June 11, 2026

The week the Collective started writing its charter. David’s proposal: a small set of values, three at most, that embody what the Ikigai Collective is about, offered so that people who join can make them their own. Explicitly a starting point, not an arrival; comment, counter-propose, discuss asynchronously, revisit next week.

The first draft: Curiosity, Compassion, Meaning.

Curiosity as the search mechanism, the way molecules test new configurations and evolution explores what is possible; the mechanism through which communities find the ideas worth building on. Compassion because iteration guarantees mistakes and failures, and these are neither shameful nor sinful; they hurt, and that is exactly why a group practicing experimentation needs to be gentle with itself and its members. Meaning as what distinguishes our way of being: “The moon is beautiful, but it has no idea why it is, no idea what it does and why it does it.”

The values got tested against reality the same night. A member whose product name had just collapsed under a trademark conflict invoked the compassion principle on himself, out loud, and moved on to the next iteration. That is what a value is for.

Also shown: a member’s AI-built industrial website that had just landed a thousand-unit order from a client who did not care that no agency was involved, only that the output met the brief; a browser-based AI rescuing a crashed WordPress site; and a thirty-dollar sensor detecting a heartbeat through Wi-Fi interference, introduced with the observation that if LIGO can measure a fraction of a proton from a hundred and thirty million light years away, your router can notice your pulse.
